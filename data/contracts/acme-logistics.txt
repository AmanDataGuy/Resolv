SUPPLY AGREEMENT — ACME LOGISTICS LTD
Master Services Agreement, Section 3: Delivery Obligations

Clause 3.1 — Notification of Delay
Supplier shall notify Buyer in writing within twenty-four (24) hours of becoming
aware of any circumstance likely to delay delivery of any order beyond the agreed
delivery window. Failure to notify within this window constitutes an independent
breach, regardless of any penalty owed under Clause 3.2.

Clause 3.2 — Late Delivery Penalty
Where Supplier fails to deliver any order by the estimated delivery date, Buyer
shall be entitled to a service credit equal to two percent (2.0%) of the shipment
value for each order affected by the delay, calculated against the total invoiced
value of the delayed shipment. This credit is Buyer's sole financial remedy for
delay under this Section, without prejudice to Buyer's right to terminate under
Section 8 for repeated or systemic delays.

Clause 3.3 — Force Majeure
Neither party shall be liable for delay caused by events beyond its reasonable
control, including natural disaster, labor action, or governmental restriction,
provided the affected party gives notice within the timeframe specified in
Clause 3.1 and uses commercially reasonable efforts to mitigate the delay.

Master Services Agreement, Section 4: Product Quality and Rejection

Clause 4.1 — Inspection Rights
Buyer may inspect any shipment within seventy-two (72) hours of receipt and
reject any unit that fails to conform to the specifications set out in Exhibit A.

Clause 4.2 — Rejection Notice and Return
Buyer shall notify Supplier of a rejection within twenty-four (24) hours of the
inspection finding. Supplier shall arrange collection of rejected goods within
five (5) business days and issue a full credit note within ten (10) business days
of collection. Repeated quality failures affecting more than five percent (5%) of
units in a rolling ninety (90) day period entitle Buyer to require a corrective
action plan under Clause 4.3.

Master Services Agreement, Section 5: Supplier Capacity

Clause 5.1 — Capacity Warranty
Supplier warrants it maintains sufficient production and fulfillment capacity to
meet Buyer's forecast order volumes as updated quarterly under Exhibit B. Where
Supplier cannot fulfill a confirmed order due to a capacity shortfall, Supplier
must notify Buyer within twelve (12) hours of identifying the shortfall so that
Buyer may source an alternate supplier for the affected volume.

Master Services Agreement, Section 6: Customs and Cross-Border Shipments

Clause 6.1 — Documentation Responsibility
For any shipment crossing an international border, Supplier is responsible for
providing complete and accurate customs documentation at least forty-eight (48)
hours prior to the scheduled ship date. A customs hold caused by incomplete or
inaccurate documentation supplied by Supplier is treated as a Supplier-caused
delay under Clause 3.2.

Master Services Agreement, Section 7: Invoicing and Payment Disputes

Clause 7.1 — Invoice Accuracy
Supplier shall issue invoices that match the corresponding Purchase Order in
unit price, quantity, and applicable discounts. Buyer may raise a price dispute
in writing within seventy-two (72) hours of receiving an invoice that does not
match the Purchase Order. Payment on the disputed line items is suspended until
the discrepancy is resolved in writing by both parties.
